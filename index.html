Satellites Constellation

We are deploying a constellation of more than ten satellites in low Earth orbit to enhance data volume and quality, providing more accurate predictive models and richer scientific data.

Real-time Early Warnings

Through preliminary onboard computations and ground-based modeling, the monitored data can be processed to provide real-time early warnings, allowing ground-based systems more time to respond and minimize potential damage.

Satellite Insurance

Insurance companies and satellite manufacturers can also utilize the data to assess the space weather conditions of mission locations, serving as a basis for satellite insurance underwriting.

Sustainability

The related data can be provided to academic institutions for research purposes or serve as a reference for satellite mission development, enhancing our understanding of space weather and improving the planning timeline of space missions.

CubeSat Subsystems Overview

Introducing the essential subsystems that make up our CubeSats, each designed for maximum efficiency and scientific reliability.

Watch more detail

Electrical Power Subsystem (EPS)

Provides and manages electrical power. Includes solar panels, batteries, and power conditioning units to ensure continuous operation.

Command and Data Handling Subsystem (C&DH)

The "brain" of the satellite. Handles command execution, data processing, health monitoring, and interface management for all other subsystems.

Telemetry, Tracking and Command Subsystem (TT&C)

Enables two-way communication with the ground station. Includes transceivers and antennae for transmitting scientific data and receiving commands.

Attitude Determination and Control Subsystem (ADCS)

Controls the satellite's orientation and position in space. Uses sensors (like sun sensor) and actuators (like reaction wheels) to point the instrument accurately.

Thermal Control Subsystem (TCS)

Maintains all components within their operational temperature range using passive (EMI) and active (heaters) methods against extreme space temperatures.

Structure and Mechanism Subsystem (SMS)

The body of the satellite. Provides mechanical support, protects internal components, and houses deployment mechanisms (e.g., for solar arrays or antennae).

Reaction Control Subsystem (RCS)

The satellite is equipped with systems for orbit and attitude adjustments, and includes a deorbit design to ensure proper disposal at the end of the mission.
